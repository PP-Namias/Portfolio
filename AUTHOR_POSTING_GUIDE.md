# 👤 Author Profile Posting Guide

## 🎯 Purpose
Create your professional author profile that will be linked to all blog posts. This is the first content you should create.

---

## 📋 Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Access Sanity Studio
1. **Go to**: https://ppnamias.sanity.studio/
2. **Sign in** with your Sanity account
3. **Verify** you're in the correct project (`gerattrr`)

### Step 2: Navigate to Authors
1. **Click**: "Authors" in the left sidebar
2. **Click**: "Create New Author" or the "+" button

### Step 3: Fill Required Information

#### **Name** (Required)
- **Value**: `PP Namias`
- **Alternative**: `Paul Patrick Namias` (if you prefer full name)

#### **Slug** (Auto-generated)
- **Will auto-create**: `pp-namias` or `paul-patrick-namias`
- **Manual edit if needed**: Keep it simple and professional

#### **Profile Image** (Recommended)
1. **Click**: "Upload" in the Image field
2. **Upload**: High-quality professional photo
3. **Requirements**:
   - Size: 400x400px (square format preferred)
   - Format: JPG or PNG
   - File size: Under 2MB
   - Professional appearance
4. **Alternative Text**: 
   - `PP Namias - Full Stack Developer and AI Solutions Engineer`

#### **Bio** (Required for SEO)
**Template:**
```
Passionate full-stack developer specializing in AI automation, chatbot development, and modern web applications. With expertise in React, Node.js, TypeScript, and AI integration, I create intelligent solutions that streamline business operations and enhance user experiences.

Currently serving as AI Developer and Team Leader at Wilshire Financial Network, where I design and deploy no-code AI chatbots and automation tools. I've led teams in developing marketing materials, CRM systems, and AI agents that transform how businesses operate.

My technical expertise spans across frontend frameworks (React, Vue.js), backend technologies (Node.js, Express), databases (PostgreSQL, MongoDB), and AI platforms (OpenAI, n8n automation). I'm particularly passionate about creating solutions that bridge the gap between complex technology and real-world business needs.
```

**Customization Guidelines:**
- Keep it between 150-300 words
- Include your key technologies
- Mention current role/projects
- Add personality while staying professional
- Include keywords for SEO

## 🎨 Content Guidelines

### Name Best Practices:
- ✅ **Use**: Professional name you want to be known by
- ✅ **Consider**: "PP Namias" for branding consistency
- ❌ **Avoid**: Nicknames or informal names
- ❌ **Avoid**: Special characters or numbers

### Bio Writing Tips:
1. **Start Strong**: Lead with your primary expertise
2. **Be Specific**: Mention exact technologies you use
3. **Show Impact**: Include what you've accomplished
4. **Personal Touch**: Add what you're passionate about
5. **Keywords**: Natural inclusion of searchable terms

### Image Requirements:
- **Professional headshot** preferred
- **Good lighting** and clear image
- **Neutral background** (white, gray, or professional setting)
- **Appropriate attire** (business casual or professional)
- **Confident expression** and direct eye contact

## 📱 Preview & Testing

### After Creating:
1. **Click**: "Preview" to see how it looks
2. **Check**: All fields are properly filled
3. **Verify**: Image displays correctly
4. **Test**: Bio length is appropriate (not too long/short)

### Publication:
1. **Click**: "Publish" button (top right)
2. **Confirm**: Publication in the dialog
3. **Wait**: For success confirmation

---

## 🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Mistakes:
- ❌ **Too Generic**: "I'm a developer who likes coding"
- ❌ **Too Long**: More than 400 words in bio
- ❌ **No Keywords**: Missing searchable terms
- ❌ **Inconsistent**: Different from other profiles

### Technical Mistakes:
- ❌ **Large Images**: Over 2MB file size
- ❌ **Poor Quality**: Blurry or pixelated photos
- ❌ **Missing Alt Text**: Hurts accessibility and SEO
- ❌ **Special Characters**: In slug or name fields
