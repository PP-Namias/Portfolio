## Design Principles

### Minimalist Composition
- **Rule of Thirds:** Strategic placement of key elements
- **Negative Space:** Embracing emptiness as design element
- **Clean Lines:** Emphasizing geometric precision
- **Single Subject:** Focus on one primary element

### Architectural Elements
- **Light and Shadow:** Natural lighting as compositional tool
- **Texture Contrast:** Smooth vs. rough surface interplay
- **Color Harmony:** Monochromatic and analogous palettes
- **Spatial Relationships:** How volumes interact with each other

## Technical Excellence

### Equipment for Architecture
- **Wide-Angle Lens:** Capturing full building perspectives
- **Tilt-Shift Capability:** Correcting perspective distortion
- **Tripod Essential:** Ensuring sharp, level compositions
- **Polarizing Filter:** Managing reflections and contrast

### Post-Processing Approach
- **Perspective Correction:** Ensuring vertical lines are true
- **Contrast Enhancement:** Emphasizing form through light/dark
- **Color Grading:** Maintaining architectural mood
- **Minimal Editing:** Preserving natural architectural character

This collection celebrates the intersection of photography and architecture, where the built environment becomes a canvas for artistic expression through careful observation and thoughtful composition.
