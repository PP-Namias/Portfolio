Pushing the boundaries of traditional photography through experimental techniques, abstract compositions, and artistic interpretation of reality.

## Artistic Philosophy

Abstract photography transforms the ordinary into extraordinary by focusing on color, form, texture, and light rather than literal representation.

## Creative Techniques

### Experimental Methods
- **Multiple Exposures:** Layering time and movement
- **Intentional Camera Movement:** Controlled blur for effect
- **Macro Abstracts:** Finding patterns in small details
- **Light Painting:** Drawing with illumination

### Digital Artistry
- **Long Exposures:** Time-based compositions
- **Focus Pulling:** Selective clarity techniques
- **Color Grading:** Mood through palette
- **Composite Imagery:** Blending reality and imagination

## Equipment & Settings

### Camera Bodies
- **Canon EOS R6 Mark II:** High-resolution sensor for detail
- **Various Lenses:** 16-35mm, 50mm, 100mm macro
- **Neutral Density Filters:** Extended exposure control
- **Polarizing Filters:** Reflection management

### Post-Processing Tools
- **Adobe Lightroom:** Color grading and exposure
- **Photoshop:** Advanced compositing
- **Creative Plugins:** Artistic effect enhancement
- **Color Calibration:** Monitor accuracy essential

## Inspiration Sources

Abstract photography draws inspiration from:
- **Modern Art Movements:** Kandinsky, Pollock influences
- **Natural Phenomena:** Weather, water, light behavior
- **Urban Architecture:** Geometric forms and shadows
- **Scientific Imagery:** Microscopy, astronomy patterns

This collection represents a journey into visual experimentation, where technical precision meets artistic vision to create images that challenge perception and invite contemplation.
